K-POP TERMS

If you're new to K-Pop, you may have heard of some words that are used within the K-Pop fandoms, such as stan or bias. Here is a list of the most common words that are used and what they mean.

Stan

A slang term that refers to an extremely devoted or enthusiastic fan of a celebrity or music group. The term is a combination of the words "stalker" and "fan". It derives from an Eminem song "Stan", which is about a young man who becomes dangerously obsessed with the rapper. The term had a negative connotation, but since then it has evolved into a positive term that is used widely by fandoms.

Bias

It often refers to a fan's favorite member in a group.

Bias Wrecker

Similar to the term "bias", but it refers to a fan's second favorite member in a group.

Trainee

It refers to someone who is training to become an idol, either in a group or a soloist, under a K-pop entertainment company. They are usually trained for several years in singing and dancing. People can become a trainee if they audition or be scouted.

Comeback

A comeback refers to when an idol or group releases new music, such as a song or an album, with different concepts or themes.

Debut

It refers to a trainee who is ready to become an idol by releasing their first official single.

Idol

The word is used for K-pop stars. However, it is often used to a K-pop artist that has debuted and is no longer a trainee.

Fanchant

Refers to phrases that fans shout before or during an idol's performance. Fanchants often include the names of the members of a group, lyrics, lines, or words specific to that artist.

Leader

Many K-pop groups are assigned positions or roles. Being a leader is one of them and they are responsible for keeping the group organized and often speaks on behalf of the group at award ceremonies or in interviews.

Visual

The term usually refers to the most attractive member in a group based on Korean beauty standards.

Center

Often refers to the "face-of-the-group" that describes the member who most often in the center of a group's choreography.

Maknae

Maknae is a Korean word that means "youngest sibling" and it is used to refer to the youngest member of the group.

Netizens

A netizen is someone who actively uses the internet to engage and participate in online platforms and communities. The term is a combination of "internet" and "citizen," meaning a "citizen of the internet."

Anti

An "anti" is someone that hates a specific idol or group. Some antis will put in extreme efforts to hate on an idol by creating fake rumors or finding evidence that can damage an idol's reputation.

Music Shows

Music shows are programs where K-pop idols perform their songs and choreography for the public. Popular music shows include M! Countdown, Music Bank, Music Core, Show Champion, The Show, and Inkigayo.

Sasaeng

Refers to fans who do not respect their idol's privacy, practically stalking them wherever they go. They make idols feel uncomfortable or unsafe in some way.

The Big Three

The Big Three refers to entertainment companies that has launched successful groups that majorly influenced the K-Pop industry: SM Entertainment, YG Entertainment and JYP Entertainment.

Aegyo

Aegyo is a Korean word that is often used to describe the cuteness of someone.

Photocard

A photocard is a collectible picture of an idol or group, typically the size of a debit card, similar to trading cards. Fans mainly collect photocards from albums, along with other inclusions.

Sub-unit

Sub-units are smaller groups within an already established K-pop group, in which gives different members a chance to showcase their talents or try different music.

Nugu

Nugu comes from a Korean word meaning "who" and it refers to groups that are unpopular or not well known.
